## 1. Dimensional Computing: The Core Idea  
Dimensional computing treats software not as a sequence of instructions, but as an **emergent structure** built from layers of meaning. Each layer (dimension) adds new capabilities, and higher layers never violate the rules of lower layers.

The nine dimensions you’re using form a progression:

1. **Point** — identity  
2. **Line** — axes, variation  
3. **Plane** — coordinates, relationships  
4. **Volume** — manifolds (continuous possibility spaces)  
5. **Object** — entities (bundles of coordinates)  
6. **Motion** — change, interpolation  
7. **Awareness** — context, neighbors, deltas  
8. **Expression** — substrates, manifestation packets  
9. **Source** — rules, identity, universe origin  

Dimensional computing is about **emergence**, not computation.  
Each dimension *extends* the previous one but never contradicts it.

## 2. Manifolds: Continuous Spaces of Meaning  
A **manifold** is a continuous space that defines *how to interpret coordinates*. It does not store values. It does not compute. It defines the *shape* of a possibility space.

### What a manifold is  
- A mapping from coordinates → meaning  
- A pure function  
- A continuous space (not discrete)  
- A definition of how something behaves, not the thing itself  

### Examples  
- A **color manifold** defines hue, saturation, brightness.  
- A **sound manifold** defines frequency, amplitude, phase.  
- A **spatial manifold** defines x, y, z.  
- A **wave manifold** defines vibration properties shared by sound and light.

### Why manifolds matter  
Manifolds give your universe **structure without storing data**.  
They are the “laws of physics” for your engine.

### Where manifolds live  
`foundation-1/volume/`

## 3. Substrates: Interpreters of Meaning  
A **substrate** takes coordinates from an entity and interprets them into a **manifestation packet**—the only thing that becomes bits and bytes.

### What a substrate does  
- Reads manifold coordinates  
- Interprets them according to rules  
- Produces a packet (pixel, sound, motion, light)  
- Never computes  
- Never stores state  
- Never overrides manifolds  

### Examples  
- Pixel substrate → outputs `{x, y, color}`  
- Sound substrate → outputs `{channel, frequency, amplitude}`  
- Motion substrate → outputs `{position, velocity}`  

### Why substrates matter  
They are the bridge between **meaning** and **hardware**.  
They turn dimensional intent into something the computer can render.

### Where substrates live  
`foundation-1/expression/`

## 4. Entities: Coordinates in Manifolds  
An **entity** is a bundle of coordinates that reference manifolds.  
Entities do not store attributes—they store *positions* in manifold space.

### Examples  
- SmartPixel (color manifold + spatial manifold)  
- SmartSound (sound manifold + temporal manifold)  
- SmartObject (multiple manifolds combined)  

### Why entities matter  
They are the “things” in your universe, but they are defined purely by their coordinates, not by stored values.

### Where entities live
